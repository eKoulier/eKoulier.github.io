Using Deep Learning to Model Bordetella Pertussis

How neural networks help to predict an outbreak of whooping cough.

Posted by eKoulier on August 24, 2018

Well, Deep Learning has become a trend, but there is a reason for this. Several fields have been revolutionized and their research has been significantly facilitated: from DNA sequencing to analyzing data from LHC, machine learning and deep learning have become an unseparable part of science.

This post is about a deep learning framework that I developed for a project in collaboration with JADS and GGD.

The background

There have been several outbreaks of Pertussis in the province of Brabant in the southern Netherlands during the past decade. Late notification of Pertussis cases hampers effective and timely public health measures to reduce further transmission. Therefore, modelling this disease and its spreading mechanism is of main importance in the outbreak detection. Here we explore whether artificial intelligence and machine learning techniques can do this effectively.
